Developed and Developing Countries Life Expectancy Correlation

This dashboard uses World Health Organisation data from 2015. The data is used to explore the health status as well as many other related factors for all countries.

Country Selector

1 in 4 are developed countries

A Developed Country has better health to that of a Developing Country. You are likely to live over 10 yrs longer.

This graph shows Adult Mortality Rates of both sexes (probability of dying between 15 and 60 years per 1000 population). There is approxiametely over 95 more per 1000 likely to die in this age bracket in the Developing World to that of a Developed World

There is a strong correlation between your life expectancy and schooling. It suggests a longer education will likely lead to a longer healthier life. It also shows that the developed world invests a lot more in education resulting indirectly to a longer life.
